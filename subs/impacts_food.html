“Food is a symbolic of love when words are inadequate.”– Alan D. Wolfelt at Childfree Foundation Uganda we ensure that no child sleeps on an empty stomach. Back in the innocent days, year 2013 before we even thought of starting Childfree Foundation Uganda, food was the main reason that prompted many orphan children to pour onto the streets of Buzirandulu Village in vain of looking for what to eat.

Having lost their parents, many children came to us seeking for something to eat, a few for starters but they later on accumulated to a sum of 15 children. With our little income from the farming business we were running then we struggled to put food onto the table for these orphaned children. We could only afford one meal a day due to inadequate support and insufficient funds.

As the saying goes “good attracts masses” more and more children continued coming, issues of feeding them continuously accumulated as well as accommodation constraints. The burden became too heavy for us to bare we thus had to seek extra help from individuals and the community at large. Our cry was heard we obtained the first sponsor from Italy who gave us 40 Euros every month. This was used to buy food for the children.

Problem was partially solved as the number of children we supported increased. We later on obtained another gold hearted sponsor who bought us land we currently using for doing subsistence farming from which food is used for feeding the orphans. Like we always stress it, the journey hasn’t been an easy one though with more donations from different people we have been able to take some baby steps forward. We seek out for more people to sponsor the cause of putting food onto at least one child’s plate.
